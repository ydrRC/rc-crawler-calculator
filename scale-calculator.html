How to Use

The scale multiplier tells you what to multiply your measurements by when converting between scales.

For example, if you have a part that's 10mm in 1:10 scale and want to know what size it should be in 1:24 scale:

• Select 1:10 as your "From Scale"
• Select 1:24 as your "To Scale"
• The multiplier shows ×0.4167
• Calculate: 10mm × 0.4167 = 4.167mm

Understanding the Results:

• Multiplier greater than 1: You're scaling UP (making things larger)
• Multiplier less than 1: You're scaling DOWN (making things smaller)
• Multiplier equals 1: Same scale, no conversion needed

Railroad Gauge Information:

• G scale (1:22.5): Large garden railroad scale
• O scale (1:43.5): Scale O trains in the United Kingdom and France
• O scale (1:45): Scale O trains in Germany, Japan, and Russia
• O scale (1:48): Scale O trains in the United States
• S scale (1:64): American Flyer trains
• HO scale (1:87): Most popular model railroad scale
• N scale (1:160): Compact model railroad scale
• Z scale (1:220): Smallest commercial model railroad scale
